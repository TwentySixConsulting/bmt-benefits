The following pages set out the detail of benefits practice across the three markets. For each benefit, we set out Birmingham Museums Trust's provision as we understand it and then describe 'typical' market practice. We've done this rather than use our standard LQ/M/UQ practice because the latter is misleading in that not all organisations will have all of the benefits listed to the level described. Therefore, to be 'below' in some areas does not necessarily mean benefits provision is deficient — the absolutely most important factor is that benefits are aligned with the needs of the (current and future) employee base.

Below each table, we've listed any interesting ideas that we came across that aren't necessarily typical market practice, but might be worth noting. Market position indicators on BMT's benefits are benchmarked primarily against the Arts & Heritage Sector, with the Wider Not-for-Profit Sector providing additional context, and Large Private Sector included for broader reference.

Current Provision — benchmarked vs Arts & Heritage Sector

Birmingham Museums Trust's Benefits

All benefits currently provided by BMT, as understood at the time of this report. Market position reflects comparison against the Arts & Heritage Sector.

Pay & Leave

Annual Leave

35 days including bank holidays (employees do not need to take bank holidays as leave). Additional 2 days after 3 years' service and a further 2 days after 5 years', giving a total of 39 days for those with 5+ years.

At market / Above with service

Pension

3% employer / 5% employee contribution via Standard Life. Staff TUPE'd from Birmingham City Council are in the West Midlands Pension Fund.

Below market — 3% employer

Sick Pay

No entitlement during probation. After probation: 60 days full pay, followed by Statutory Sick Pay only.

Partially below market — no half pay period

Maternity Pay

First 6 weeks at average weekly earnings; weeks 7–18 SMP plus half pay; weeks 19 onwards SMP only.

At market

Paternity Pay

2 weeks statutory paternity pay plus 1 additional week (5 days), to be taken within 12 months of the birth.

Above market — 3 weeks total

Health & Wellbeing

Life Assurance

Group life assurance at 3× salary.

At market — 3× salary

Employee Assistance Programme

Wellbeing and Employee Support Programme — two versions available to staff.

Above market — dual provision

Health Cash Plan

Optional cash healthcare plan available to staff via BHSF, covering dental, optical and therapy claims.

At market

Mental Health First Aiders

In-house Mental Health First Aiders embedded across the organisation.

Above market

Flu Jabs & Eye Care

Flu jab newly introduced alongside statutory eye test vouchers for eligible staff.

At market

Additional & Unique Benefits

Café Discount

50% staff discount at BMT cafés, plus a free daily hot drink at sites with a café.

Unique benefit

Reciprocal Museum Admission

Free reciprocal admission to National Museums for BMT staff.

Unique benefit

Funded Development

Development opportunities funded subject to budget, including training courses and conferences.

At market
